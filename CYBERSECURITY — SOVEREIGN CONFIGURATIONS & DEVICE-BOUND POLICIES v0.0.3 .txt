Preamble

Modern cybersecurity relies on software policies, probabilistic threat models, centrally interpreted logs, and identity systems vulnerable to synthetic manipulation. The sovereign CYBERSECURITY framework replaces these fragile architectures with immutable physical-digital truth anchored to device lineage, PD-chain evidence, harmonic law, and constitutional governance. Security policies become device-bound, identity-bound, and immune to synthetic alteration. Cybersecurity becomes sovereign, not statistical.

Article 1 — Introduction

Conventional cybersecurity systems fail because they depend on:
- passwords and digital credentials without physical proof
- cloud-based policy enforcement vulnerable to tampering
- synthetic or AI-generated access attempts
- unverifiable logs and timestamps
- mutable configurations lacking lineage
- probabilistic detection rather than truth-based verification

The sovereign cybersecurity model ensures all actions originate from lawful physical identity and sealed hardware.

Article 2 — Device-bound security policies

Sovereign devices enforce:
- non-exportable private keys

- PD-chain packet requirements for all actions

- constitutional routing rules

Policies cannot be copied, altered, or synthetically recreated.

Synthetic or remote-only identity attacks become impossible.

Logs become immutable truth-records, not editable files.

Arbitration makes security deterministic, not interpretive.
