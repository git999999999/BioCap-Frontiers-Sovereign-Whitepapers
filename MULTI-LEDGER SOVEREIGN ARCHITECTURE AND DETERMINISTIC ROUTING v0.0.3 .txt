Article 1 — Introduction

Conventional blockchains operate as a single global ledger, mixing unrelated domains such as identity, financial transactions, sensor data, and program logic. This creates ambiguity, security risks, and interpretive drift. The sovereign architecture resolves these flaws by establishing multiple independent ledgers, each dedicated to capturing a specific truth-domain. Ledger independence prevents contamination; deterministic routing prevents misplacement.

Ledgers operate as sovereign truth containers. Their separation is a constitutional requirement, not a design preference.
